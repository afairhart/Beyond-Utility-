The Opportunity
Why Water, Why Now

Water infrastructure is being
rebuilt from the ground up.

  • Aging infrastructure across the developed world requires trillions in reinvestment over the next 25 years.
  • Regulatory pressure on PFAS, lead, and emerging contaminants is creating urgent demand for new treatment technologies.
  • Climate variability is driving water scarcity, flooding, and distribution challenges at an accelerating pace.
  • Water utilities are increasingly open to adopting technology — a significant shift from the historically conservative sector.

$1T+

Estimated U.S. water infrastructure investment needed over the next 25 years (EPA & ASCE estimates).

50-Year Horizon

Water infrastructure will look dramatically different. The companies building that future are being founded now.

Underserved by VC

Water technology receives a fraction of climate-tech venture capital despite massive market opportunity.

Process
Investment Process

Rigorous, domain-informed diligence.

1

Initial Screening

Problem clarity, water sector alignment, and founder-market fit. Quick pass/proceed decision.

2

Team Evaluation

Informal references, domain expertise assessment, leadership qualities, and adaptability signals.

3

Technical Review

In-person demo and deep-dive on technology, IP strategy, and competitive landscape. External experts engaged as needed.

4

Market Validation

Customer willingness to pay, regulatory tailwinds, market sizing, and go-to-market credibility.

5

Economics & Traction

Unit economics, documented evidence toward commercial traction, even at pre-revenue stage.

6

Legal & Structure

Cap table review, IP ownership, corporate structure, and term alignment.

7

Downside Analysis

What kills this company? Regulatory risk, technology risk, market timing, team risk — and mitigants for each.

Most Common Pass

Lack of a highly believable go-to-market strategy. We need to see documented steps — not just a TAM slide — showing the founder understands their path to first revenue.
