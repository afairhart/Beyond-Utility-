Key Assumptions & Methodology

Fund Structure

$12.5M fund with a 2% annual management fee over 10 years, yielding $2.5M in fees and $10M investable capital. Capital is deployed over a 3-year investment period, split approximately 50/50 between first checks and follow-on reserves.

Investment Strategy

12 pre-seed investments of $400k each into water technology companies ($4.8M deployed), with 4 new investments per year across years 1–3. The remaining investable capital is reserved for concentrated follow-on into the top 3 performers.

Follow-On Approach

The 3 strongest performers receive significant follow-on capital at the Seed or Series A stage (modeled at Year 3, once early cohorts show traction). Follow-on concentration reflects conviction—fewer bets, larger checks into proven winners.

Outcome Distribution

Gross return multiples follow a power-law: one breakout winner (50x), strong performers (25x, 15x, 10x), moderate returns (5x, 3x, 2x), capital-return deals (1x), and total losses (0x). Two of twelve companies fail entirely. Net multiples are reduced by dilution.

Dilution

20% ownership dilution per subsequent funding round, with 3 rounds assumed between pre-seed entry and exit (Seed, Series A, Series B). Non-follow-on companies retain 51.2% of initial ownership; follow-on companies retain 64.0% by participating pro-rata in one round. This is consistent with industry benchmarks for early-stage water technology companies.

Exit Assumptions

All exits modeled at Year 10 for simplicity. In practice, exits will be staggered across years 5–12 via M&A, secondary sales, or IPO.

IRR Methodology

Net IRR computed via Newton-Raphson with bisection fallback over annual cashflows. The staggered deployment across years 1–3 is reflected in the cashflow timing. Management fee drag is included as annual outflows. Reflects the LP’s time-value-adjusted net return over the fund life.
