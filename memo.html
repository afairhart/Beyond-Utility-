Section 2

Market Opportunity

The global water sector is experiencing a once-in-a-generation transformation driven by aging infrastructure, regulatory expansion, climate variability, and growing acceptance of technology-driven solutions by historically conservative water utilities.

Macro Drivers

Aging infrastructure. The American Society of Civil Engineers and the EPA estimate that the United States alone requires over $1 trillion in water infrastructure investment over the next 25 years. Pipes, treatment facilities, and distribution systems installed mid-20th century are reaching end of life, creating massive demand for both replacement and modernization.

Regulatory expansion. New and emerging regulations around PFAS, lead, microplastics, and other contaminants of concern are creating urgent, compliance-driven demand for new treatment technologies. Utilities must invest regardless of budget constraints — regulatory mandates are non-discretionary spend.

Climate variability. Droughts, flooding, saltwater intrusion, and shifting precipitation patterns are disrupting water availability and quality assumptions that have underpinned water system design for decades. Adaptation requires new monitoring, treatment, and distribution technologies.

Utility technology adoption. Water utilities are increasingly open to piloting and adopting technology solutions. Digital water, smart metering, remote monitoring, and AI-driven operations are moving from early adopter to mainstream adoption. This shift dramatically improves the go-to-market environment for water tech startups.

Underserved by venture capital. Despite a market measured in the trillions, water technology receives a small fraction of the venture capital directed toward energy, mobility, and other climate-tech verticals. This creates a structurally favorable environment for a specialized fund: less competition for deals, more reasonable entry valuations, and founders who are underserved by the existing investor ecosystem.

Why Early-Stage Water Tech

The best water technology companies often start in university labs, research institutions, or with PhD-trained founders addressing problems they've observed firsthand. These founders are technically exceptional but frequently lack business experience, industry connections, and understanding of the specific go-to-market challenges unique to selling into utilities and industrial water users. A specialized investor who can provide domain-specific support from the earliest stages has a meaningful structural advantage in both deal access and value creation.

Investment Strategy & Process

Thesis

The fund invests at the Pre-Seed and Seed stages in companies developing water and wastewater technologies. The evaluation framework weights approximately 70% market opportunity (problem urgency, market size, regulatory tailwind, customer willingness to pay) and 30% financial metrics (unit economics, go-to-market credibility, documented traction). The fund invests in pre-revenue companies where we have conviction in the founder, the technology, and the market.

Selection Criteria

  • Market domain expertise — Does the founder deeply understand the water problem they're solving?
  • Team traits — Leadership, adaptability, and domain credibility. 50–75% of investments expected to be first-time founders.
  • Market size & urgency — Is there a large, growing, or regulation-driven market?
  • Believable go-to-market — Documented steps toward traction, not just a TAM slide. This is the most common reason we pass on otherwise promising companies.
  • IP strategy — Strong defensibility through patents, proprietary data, or technical moats.

The fund targets a concentrated portfolio of 10–20 pre-seed and seed stage investments, with typical initial check sizes of approximately $500,000 targeting ~7.5% ownership. Exit pathways in water technology include strategic acquisitions by large utilities, industrial conglomerates, and established water technology companies, as well as follow-on funding rounds led by later-stage investors in our co-investment network.
